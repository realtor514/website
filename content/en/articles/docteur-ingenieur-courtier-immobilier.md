People often raise an eyebrow when they hear my background: doctorate in biomedical engineering from ÉTS, five years of university teaching, biomedical equipment management for communities in Nunavik, and now real estate broker at RE/MAX DU CARTIER INC.

The instinct is to see a contradiction. I see a straight line.

## The Research Mindset Applied to Real Estate

During my doctoral work, I spent years learning to do one thing with precision: analyze complex data and translate it into clear conclusions for non-specialist audiences. I finished second nationally in the *Ma thèse en 180 secondes* competition, a challenge that asks researchers to explain five years of doctoral work to a general audience in three minutes.

That competition taught me something I use every day as a broker: the ability to make the complex accessible without making it inaccurate. Real estate is full of complexity: market cycles, mortgage mechanics, legal conditions, tax implications, building systems. Most of it is explained poorly or not at all to the people who need it most.

I wanted to change that.

## The Purchase That Changed My Direction

In 2022, I bought a triplex in Laval.

I approached it like a research project. I analyzed comparable sales, built a full cash flow model, assessed the building systematically, and stress-tested the numbers against different scenarios.

As I went through the process, people around me started asking questions. Colleagues, family members, friends in the Lebanese-Canadian community. "How do you know if the price is fair?" "How do you calculate the rental yield?" "What happens if the tenant doesn't pay?"

I realized that the information existed, but it was scattered, technical, and largely inaccessible to the people making some of the most significant financial decisions of their lives. Most of them were going through a process they didn't fully understand, guided by professionals they couldn't fully evaluate.

That realization is what brought me to the real estate industry.

## What Engineering Actually Brings to Brokerage

I'm not the only person with a technical background who has entered real estate, but the combination is still rare, and it shows up concretely in the way I work.

**Data-first analysis:** When I evaluate a property's price, I build a comparables analysis the way I would build an experiment: controlling for variables, adjusting for differences, identifying outliers. I don't tell clients a price "feels right." I show them the data and explain my reasoning.

**Building literacy:** A doctorate in biomedical engineering involves understanding mechanical and electrical systems. I can look at a building's plumbing age, electrical panel type, and HVAC condition and understand what I'm seeing, not as a replacement for a professional inspector, but as a first filter that helps my clients know what questions to ask.

**Risk management:** Research teaches you to identify risks, quantify them, and make decisions under uncertainty without pretending certainty doesn't exist. In real estate transactions, things go wrong. My job is to anticipate them, not just react to them.

**Pedagogy:** Five years of university teaching sharpens the ability to meet someone where they are and explain concepts in terms that make sense to them. I have explained mortgage stress tests to engineers and to recent immigrants with no Canadian financial background. The explanation adapts; the accuracy doesn't.

## The Nunavik Chapter

Before becoming a broker, I managed biomedical equipment for healthcare facilities serving communities in Nunavik, Quebec's far north. That role involved coordinating critical resources under severe logistical constraints, managing significant budgets with tight margins, and navigating complex stakeholder relationships.

In hindsight, it was excellent training for real estate transactions.

Managing a property purchase involves coordinating timelines, budgets, inspectors, notaries, mortgage brokers, and two parties with opposing interests, all under deadline pressure. The project management instincts developed in Nunavik translate directly.
